⚗️ Biosynthesis Pathway: From Cholesterol to Sex Hormones

Sex hormones are derived from cholesterol and regulated by gonadotropins [[4]]. The conversion involves a complex multienzyme process [[29]].

1

Cholesterol P450scc

Starting material - 27 carbon steroid that enters mitochondria

2

Pregnenolone 3β-HSD

First steroid formed - 21 carbons (pregnane)

3

Progesterone 17α-OH

Progestin - can convert to androgens or corticosteroids

4

Androstenedione 17,20-lyase

Androgen precursor - 19 carbons (androstane)

5

Testosterone 5α-reductase / Aromatase

Primary androgen - converts to DHT or Estrogen

6

Estradiol (E2) Aromatase

Primary estrogen - synthesized from testosterone [[3]]

📚 Key Facts

  • Steroid hormones are synthesized in the gonads, adrenal glands, placenta, and even in the nervous system [[6]]
  • The process involves conversion of cholesterol (27 carbons) to pregnane (21 carbons), then to androstane (19 carbons) [[8]]
  • Estrogen is synthesized by aromatization of androgens (testosterone & androstenedione) [[5]]
  • Testosterone can be metabolized to DHT by 5α-reductase [[3]]
  • Regulation begins at the hypothalamus with GnRH release [[13]]
